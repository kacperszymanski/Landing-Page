Onion Rings

Description:

Onion rings are a popular fast food and casual dining item, consisting of sliced onions that have been coated in a seasoned flour or batter and deep-fried until crispy and golden brown. They are often served as a side dish or appetizer, and can be accompanied by a variety of dipping sauces, such as ranch, ketchup, or barbecue sauce. The onions are typically sliced into thick or thin rings, depending on the recipe or personal preference. The coating can range from a simple flour or breadcrumb mixture to a more complex batter, which can include ingredients such as beer or buttermilk. Onion rings can be made with any type of onion, but sweet onions are particularly well suited for this dish because of their natural sweetness and mild flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup caramel color
  • 1/2 cup caffeine powder
  • 1/2 cup phosphoric acid
  • 1/4 cup vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup Coca-Cola syrup

Steps:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the sugar, caramel color, caffeine powder, phosphoric acid, vanilla extract, lime juice, and water.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the sugar has dissolved.
  3. Remove from heat and add the Coca-Cola syrup.
  4. Allow to cool and then transfer to a bottle or pitcher.
  5. To serve, add the mixture to a glass filled with ice and add carbonated water to taste.
